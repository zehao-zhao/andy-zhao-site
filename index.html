About Me

I’m Andy, a Ph.D. student at the University of Southern California advised by Professor Rahul Jain. My research interests lie in approximation algorithms and reinforcement learning for complex decision‑making. Currently I’m developing a simplex‑based approximation algorithm for mixed‑integer optimization (MIO) under the guidance of Professor Dimitris Bertsimas.

Previously, I earned my M.S. in Business Analytics & Operations Research at the MIT Operations Research Center and Sloan School of Management, where I explored large‑scale optimization and data‑driven decision‑making. Before that I completed quadruple degrees (B.A./B.S.) in Computer Science (EECS), Data Science, Pure Mathematics and Statistics at the University of California, Berkeley. I was fortunate to be mentored by Professor James Demmel on eigenfaces and federated learning, by Professor Saul Perlmutter on the Public Editor Project, and by Dr. Jiahao Yao on machine learning for finance.

Driven by curiosity and an entrepreneurial spirit, I blend rigorous analytical research with product development. I design resource‑efficient algorithms for embedded devices, optimize large language models and recommender systems, and build AI‑driven products from 0‑to‑1. My goal is to translate complex theory into systems that empower people and create real‑world impact.
